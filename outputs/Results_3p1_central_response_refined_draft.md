## D. Results 3.1 draft

The central response was first evaluated from the main scan over \(T=0.90\text{--}2.00\,\mathrm{s}\) with \(\Delta T=0.01\,\mathrm{s}\), using the same five central probes and the same normalization for \(N=4\), \(N=6\), and \(N=8\). A refined short-period scan over \(T=0.85\text{--}1.05\,\mathrm{s}\) with \(\Delta T=0.005\,\mathrm{s}\) was then used to clarify the lower-boundary peak issue observed in the main scan. Figure X gives the main-scan central response, while Figure Y and Table X provide the refined short-period peak identification. In all cases, \(A^*(x,y;T)\), \(A_{c,\mathrm{mean}}\), and \(A_{c,\max}\) denote incident-amplitude-normalized frequency-domain complex-amplitude magnitudes, not time-domain wave heights.

For the mean central response, the refined scan shows a monotonic increase in the peak value as the number of cylinders increases. The peak \(A_{c,\mathrm{mean}}\) is 1.139445 at \(T=0.955\,\mathrm{s}\) for \(N=4\), 1.238387 at \(T=0.970\,\mathrm{s}\) for \(N=6\), and 1.355463 at \(T=0.995\,\mathrm{s}\) for \(N=8\). Thus, under the present fixed-body diffraction model and common probe definition, increasing \(N\) strengthens the averaged central response over the refined short-period interval. The corresponding peak periods also shift slightly toward longer periods from \(N=4\) to \(N=8\).

The maximum central response exhibits the same ordering in peak magnitude, but the short-period peak locations require more careful interpretation. The refined scan clarifies the main-scan lower-boundary peak issue for \(N=4\) and \(N=6\). For \(N=4\), the refined peak of \(A_{c,\max}\) is 1.184359 at \(T=0.870\,\mathrm{s}\), which is close to the lower-boundary inspection interval and should therefore be interpreted cautiously. For \(N=6\), the refined peak is 1.285190 at \(T=0.905\,\mathrm{s}\), while for \(N=8\), the refined peak is 1.418618 at \(T=0.945\,\mathrm{s}\). The \(N=6\) and \(N=8\) peaks are interior peaks within the refined scan, whereas the \(N=4\) result remains more sensitive to the lower end of the inspected period range.

Under the present linear potential-flow BEM model and unified normalization, increasing \(N\) enhances the central response in both averaged and local-maximum indicators. However, short-period peak interpretation, especially for \(N=4\), remains sensitive to the lower end of the refined scan. The results should therefore be read as frequency-dependent central response trends for the specified fixed arrays and probe definitions, rather than as evidence for a labelled modal mechanism.
