# 3.4 Representative spatial distributions of the normalized free-surface response

Sections 3.1-3.3 examined the fixed circular-cylinder ring arrays using probe-based response measures. The present section supplements those point-response results with representative two-dimensional field maps for selected periods, without extending the analysis to a separate array-size field comparison. The plotted quantity is the incident-amplitude-normalized free-surface response magnitude, defined as A*(x,y;T)=|eta_total(x,y;T)|/A_inc, where eta_total is the complex total free-surface elevation and A_inc is the incident-wave amplitude. These maps are therefore interpreted as representative spatial visualizations of the frequency-domain response-magnitude field, not as time-domain wave-height snapshots.

Figure V1 presents the N=8 response-magnitude fields at T=0.98, 1.00, 1.02, and 1.40 s. These four maps are used as the confirmed representative field figures for this section: figures/field_abs_N8_Tp098.png, figures/field_abs_N8_Tp100.png, figures/field_abs_N8_Tp102.png, and figures/field_abs_N8_Tp140.png. The selected periods provide spatial context for the probe-based trends discussed in the preceding subsections while keeping the interpretation limited to the fixed-body diffraction cases considered here.

At the shorter periods T=0.98, 1.00, and 1.02 s, the maps show an elevated response-magnitude region within and immediately around the central part of the ring. The response is not spatially uniform across this central region or its surroundings, and the elevated region extends mildly toward the rear side. This spatial pattern is qualitatively consistent with the central-response enhancement reported in Section 3.1 and with the probe-dependent non-uniformity discussed in Section 3.2.

At T=1.40 s, the response-magnitude distribution is smoother and less concentrated around the ring center than in the shorter-period maps. The spatial contrast is weaker and the elevated response is more diffuse, indicating that the normalized free-surface response magnitude varies with the selected period rather than remaining spatially invariant.

Overall, the N=8 representative field maps provide spatial visualization support for the probe-based results in Sections 3.1-3.3. They help identify where elevated and non-uniform response magnitudes appear in the computational domain for selected periods. They should not be used alone to claim a confirmed modal structure, resonance mechanism, response-reduction mechanism, or any energy-based transmission property.
